Barely Touching Reality

Schizophrenia is a chronic and severe mental disorder characterized by recurring psychosis, or detachment from reality. Schizophrenia also affects how one thinks, feels, and behave. The symptoms of schizophrenia fall into three categories: positive, negative, and cognitive.

Positive symptoms are symptoms that are associated with a psychotic episode. These include:

  • Hallucinations
  • Delusions
  • Disordered thought and speech

Negative symptoms are symptoms that are associated with deficit in thought, feeling, and behavior. These include:

  • Poverty of speech
  • Showing little emotions
  • Lack of social interation
  • Lack of self-care
  • Lack of motivation and apathy

Cognitive symptoms are symptoms associated with deficits in cognition. These include:

  • Deficit in attention, memory, and reasoning
  • Decreased social cognition
  • Decreased level of semantic processing

It should be of note that people with schizophrenia often do not display all these symptoms and that each person's experience with schizophrenia is different.
